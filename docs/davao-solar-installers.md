## DOE Accreditation Notes

- The Philippine Department of Energy (DOE) maintains an **Official Solar PV Installer Registry** updated periodically. The most recent versions found were dated **October 30, 2024**.
- Registry PDF available at: [DOE Solar PV Installer Registry (Oct 2024)](https://prod-cms.doe.gov.ph/documents/d/eumb/doe_solar_pv_installer_registry_as_of_30_october_2024)
- **Requirements for DOE accreditation:** At least 3 years continuous hands-on experience in solar PV installation/O&M; proof of at least 3 completed solar projects in the past 3 years; certificate valid for 3 years and renewable.
- Among the companies listed above, only **Sunstruck Solar Solutions** explicitly advertises DOE accreditation on their website. Others may be registered but do not prominently display it. To verify, check the DOE registry PDF linked above.

## PSSEA (Philippine Solar and Storage Energy Alliance) Notes

- PSSEA was established in 2010 as a non-profit uniting manufacturers, developers, contractors, funders, rooftop installers, and energy off-takers.
- Website: [pssea.ph](https://pssea.ph/)
- A full public member directory was not found online. Known members include national-scale companies like Solar Philippines, SunPower Philippines, First Gen, ACEN, etc.
- None of the Davao-based companies above were confirmed as PSSEA members, though some may be. Contact PSSEA's secretariat for verification.
